QAITBAY CITADEL

Qaitbay Citadel is one of the most important historical fortresses in Egypt, located in Alexandria on the Mediterranean coast. It was built in the 15th century during the Mamluk era by Sultan Al-Ashraf Abu Al-Nasr Qaitbay, who aimed to strengthen Egypt’s coastal defenses against increasing naval threats, especially from the rising Ottoman power. The citadel was constructed in 1477 AD on the northern tip of Alexandria in the Ras El Tin area, a highly strategic location that once held the famous Lighthouse of Alexandria, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. It was built using local limestone and stones believed to have been taken from the ruins of the ancient lighthouse.

Location
Alexandria, Egypt

SALADIN CITADEL

The Citadel of Saladin (Saladin Citadel) is one of the most important historical fortresses in Egypt and the Islamic world, located in Cairo on the Mokattam Hill, which gives it a highly strategic elevated position overlooking the entire city. It was built in the 12th century during the rule of Sultan Saladin (Al-Nasir Salah al-Din), the founder of the Ayyubid dynasty, who constructed it around 1176 AD to protect Cairo from potential Crusader attacks and to strengthen the defense of the newly established Ayyubid state. The location was carefully chosen because its height made it extremely difficult to attack and allowed full control and surveillance of the city.

Location
Cairo, Egypt

SHALI FORTRESS

Shali Fortress is a historic mudbrick citadel located in Siwa Oasis in the Western Desert of Egypt. It was founded in the 13th century, around 1203 AD, by the local Berber (Amazigh) inhabitants of Siwa as a fortified settlement to protect the community from raids and harsh desert conditions. The fortress was built mainly from kershef, a traditional material made of salt, clay, and mud, which was well-suited to the oasis environment but vulnerable to rain damage. Shali was designed as a compact, multi-story structure with narrow streets and tightly packed houses, allowing it to function both as a defensive stronghold and a residential town.

Location
Siwa Oasis, Egypt

ARISH FORTRESS

The Arish Fortress (also known as El Arish Castle) is a historic coastal fort located in the city of Arish in North Sinai, Egypt, overlooking the Mediterranean Sea. It was originally built as a strategic defensive stronghold to protect Egypt’s eastern borders and control the important land and sea routes between Egypt and the Levant. The fortress dates back to the medieval period and was later used and modified during the Mamluk and Ottoman eras, reflecting its long-standing military importance. Because of its location on the Sinai coast, it played a key role in monitoring movements along trade and invasion routes.

Location
North Sinai, Egypt
